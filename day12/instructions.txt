Objective: Become proficient with 2d arrays by simulating Conway's Game of Life.

Conway's Game of Life is an example of cellular automata. It is represented as a 2 dimensional plane comprised of cells. A cell is either alive or dead. Cells adjacent to each other are considered neighbors.

The rules are simple:

    A live cell remains alive if it is surrounded by 2 or 3 live neighbors
    A live cell dies if it is surrounded by less than 2 live neighbors (underpopulation) or more than 3 live neighbors (overpopulation)
    A dead cell is reborn into a live cell if it is surrounded by exactly 3 live neighbors
